Week 2 · Build Your AI Assistant

A practical follow-along session where you build a chatbot connected to company knowledge, then adapt it into a useful business assistant for a real team scenario.

📅 Date: Mar 20
🧭 Format: 3 guided builds
⏱️ Total Time: 60 min
Instructor: Xinran Liu

Expected outcome

Participants can build a functional chatbot connected to company knowledge and adapt it to a real business use case.

Menu

Follow along, click to expand, and build as you go.

1
15 min

Build a Copilot Agent Connected to Company Knowledge

Watch a live demo of building a Copilot agent that answers questions using information from a finance folder in SharePoint.

What you will see
  • How to connect Copilot to a specific SharePoint site or folder containing finance documents.
  • How the agent searches those files and answers questions based on the available content.
  • How to test the agent with realistic finance-related questions.
Copilot vs ChatGPT
  • Copilot can connect directly to SharePoint and use live company data.
  • ChatGPT currently cannot connect to SharePoint live and relies on uploaded files.
  • Live connection for ChatGPT is expected to be enabled in the next few weeks.
Why connect to a specific SharePoint site

Connecting Copilot to a specific SharePoint site or folder improves accuracy. It narrows the search space so the agent focuses only on the most relevant documents instead of searching too broadly.

Practice questions
What does the finance policy say about expense approval limits?
Summarize the travel reimbursement process in 3 bullet points.
Which finance documents mention approval before payment submission?
2
15 min

Build a ChatGPT Agent for Document Generation

Watch a live demo of building a ChatGPT agent that generates a document in a specific format based on an uploaded file.

What you will see
  • How to upload a file and use it as source material for an agent.
  • How to instruct the agent to produce output in a required format, such as a summary, memo, report, or FAQ.
  • How small changes to instructions improve consistency and formatting.
Sample instruction
Based on the uploaded file, generate a one-page summary in this format: background, key points, recommended actions, and next steps. Keep the tone professional and concise.
Tips
  • Be specific about the exact structure you want.
  • State what the agent should include and what it should avoid.
  • Test with different file types and compare the outputs.
3
30 min

Build Your Own Agent

Use the second half of the session to build something yourself on the spot, test it, and ask questions as you go.

What to build

Choose a simple use case that is relevant to your team and try building it during the session.

Ideas by department
  • HR: onboarding assistant, policy Q&A bot, training recommender
  • Sales: sales FAQ bot, proposal drafting assistant, customer meeting prep
  • Finance: expense policy assistant, invoice or PO query bot, budget explainer
  • IT: helpdesk assistant, troubleshooting guide, system access helper
  • Analytics: dashboard explainer, SQL helper, data dictionary bot
Good starting point
Pick one repeated question your team gets often, gather the documents that answer it, and build a simple agent around that use case.
Tips
  • Start narrow so your agent feels focused and useful.
  • Bring one or two good documents instead of too many files.
  • Test with realistic questions and refine the instructions based on the answers.
  • Use the live Q&A time to troubleshoot and improve your build.
