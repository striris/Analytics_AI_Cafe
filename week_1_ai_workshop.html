AI Settings & Security

Learn the key ChatGPT settings, what they mean, and how to configure them safely. You will explore style settings, memory, and custom instructions, then test how small changes affect AI responses.

Key settings explained
  • Base style and tone: Controls how ChatGPT writes responses. For example you can make it more formal, concise, or enthusiastic.
  • Characteristics: Optional style adjustments such as warmth, enthusiasm, use of headers, or emojis.
  • Memory: Allows ChatGPT to remember preferences about you over time so responses become more personalized.
  • Custom instructions: A place where you tell ChatGPT permanent preferences about how it should respond.
Example custom instruction

You can add simple rules that make responses easier to read or match your organization's writing style.

Do not use em-dash in responses.
Security tip

Avoid using personal ChatGPT Pro or free accounts for company work. These accounts are not managed by your organization and may store or train on your inputs. When you paste internal documents, meeting notes, or data into a personal AI tool, you may accidentally expose sensitive company information.

Instead, use your organization's approved AI tools where security, privacy, and data policies are managed by the company.

Copilot for Meetings

Use Copilot to automatically generate meeting notes and action items from recorded meetings.

Detailed instructions
  • When running a meeting, start recording so Copilot can capture the discussion.
  • Let everyone know the meeting is being recorded.
  • Invite or enable the AI facilitator so Copilot can generate notes, action items, and follow‑up tasks.
  • After the meeting, review the generated notes and check whether decisions and owners are correct.
  • You can also download the transcript and paste it into another AI tool to create customized summaries or reports.
Tips
  • State decisions clearly during the meeting so the AI captures them accurately.
  • Use the transcript if you want to generate different summary formats for different audiences.
  • Do a quick human review before sharing meeting notes widely.

Prompt Like a Pro

Demonstrate structured prompting techniques. Participants practice rewriting weak prompts into stronger ones and test the difference in results live.

How to prompt like a pro
  • Give a clear role: Tell the AI who it should act as, such as a trainer, analyst, or meeting assistant.
  • State the task: Be specific about what you want created, summarized, rewritten, or analyzed.
  • Add context: Include audience, purpose, tone, and any background information that matters.
  • Set constraints: Define length, format, style, and what to include or avoid.
  • Ask for structure: Request bullets, tables, steps, or sections so the output is easy to use.
Exercise

Rewrite this weak prompt into a stronger one:

“Help me write meeting notes.”
Sample answer
You are an executive assistant. Turn this meeting transcript into clear meeting notes for a busy project team. Include: 1) a 3-bullet summary, 2) key decisions made, 3) action items with owner and deadline, and 4) risks or follow-ups. Keep the tone professional and concise.
